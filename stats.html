How Address Derivation Works

  1. You connect your EVM wallet (MetaMask, WTX EVM, or any Web3 wallet).
  2. You sign the message "WATTx Merged Mining Wallet Derivation v1". This is a read-only signature — no transaction, no gas, no funds moved.
  3. The 65-byte signature is hashed with keccak256 to produce a 32-byte master seed.
  4. A BIP32 HD wallet is derived from that seed. Each chain gets its own BIP44 derivation path (e.g. m/44'/0'/0'/0/0 for BTC).
  5. EVM chains (ALT, OCTA, WTX) use your connected address directly — no separate key needed.
  6. XMR uses Ed25519 derivation: the spend key is derived from keccak256(seed ∥ "XMR") reduced mod the ed25519 group order.
